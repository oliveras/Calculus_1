Problem 1 (15 points)

For \(\displaystyle f(x) = 9x^3 - 4x\), find

  • the intervals where \(f(x)\) is increasing and decreasing,
  • the intervals where \(f(x)\) is concave up and concave down,
  • the \(x\) value corresponding to any local extrema,
  • the \(x\) value corresponding to any inflection points.

You must show your work.  Answers with no supporting details will receive zero credit.

Helpful reference: Chapter 4 - Study/Review Material - see the first and second derivative test.

Important Note:
Please note that there was a typo on the original version of these solutions.  The original solution incorrectly stated that the critical numbers were at \(x = \pm\frac{2}{3}\).  The critical numbers are located at \(x = \pm \frac{2}{3\sqrt{3}}\).
The above solution has been updated and is correct.

Problem 8 (14 points)

Using differentiation rules, determine the requested derivative. You do not need to simplify your final answer, but you must show your work.  Answers with no supporting details will receive zero credit.

Helpful reference for parts (a) - (c):
Chapter 3 - Study/Review Materials for differentiation rules

 

a) \(\displaystyle f(x) = \frac{x^2 - 3x + 1}{\sec(x)}\), find \(f'(x)\)

b) \(\displaystyle x(t) = \tan\left(t^2\sqrt{t^2+1}\right)\), find \(\displaystyle \dot{x}(t)\)

 

c) \(\displaystyle y = xe^{\cos(x)}\ln\left(\sqrt{x^2 + 2x+1}\right)\), find \(\displaystyle \frac{dy}{dx}\)

Note: if you didn't simplify first, your unsimplified answer may take one of the following forms (both of which are correct) :

\[\frac{dy}{dx}=e^{\cos(x)} \cdot \ln\left(\sqrt{x^2 + 2x + 1}\right) -x \cdot  \sin(x) e^{\cos(x)} \cdot \ln\left(\sqrt{x^2 + 2x + 1}\right) + x \cdot e^{\cos(x)} \cdot \frac{1}{\sqrt{x^2 + 2x + 1}}\cdot \frac{1}{2}\cdot\frac{2x + 2}{\sqrt{x^2 + 2x + 1}}\]

or 

\[\frac{dy}{dx}=\frac{1}{2} \cdot e^{\cos(x)} \cdot \ln\left(x^2 + 2 x + 1\right) -\frac{1}{2} \cdot x  \cdot \sin(x) \cdot  e^{\cos(x)} \cdot \ln\left(x^2 + 2x + 1\right) + \frac{1}{2} \cdot x \cdot e^{\cos(x)} \cdot \frac{2x + 2}{x^2 + 2x + 1}\]
