Problem 1 - Critical Numbers, Local Extrema, and Absolute Extrema

Find the critical points and the local and absolute extrema of \(f(x) = 3x^4 - 4x^3 - 12x^2 + 6\) over \(\left[-3,3\right]\).

Problem 2 - Related Rates

Two airplanes are flying in the air at the same height: airplane \(A\) is flying east at 250 mi/h and airplane \(B\) is flying north at 300mi/h. If they are both heading to the same airport, located 30 miles east of airplane \(A\) and 40 miles north of airplane \(B\), at what rate is the distance between the airplanes changing?

Hint: you may find the following link helpful: Reference: Formulas from Geometry

Problem 3 - Applied Optimization Problem

A window is composed of a semicircle placed on top of a rectangle. If you have 20ft of window-framing materials for the outer frame, what is the maximum size of the window you can create? Use \(r\) to represent the radius of the semicircle.

Problem 4 - Limits at \(\infty\) and L’Hôpital’s Rule

Determine the following limits:

a) \(\displaystyle \lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{3x^2 + 7x - 2}{3x - 7x^2 + 1}\)

b) \(\displaystyle \lim_{x\to 0}\frac{\sin(x) - \tan(x)}{x^3}\)

jump to solution

Problem 5 - Antiderivatives

Find the antiderivative \(F(x)\) to \(f(x)\) that satisfies the given condition.

a) \(f(x) = 3x^2 - 8x + 1\) with \(F(1) = 2\).

b) \(f(x) = 7\sec^2(x) + \frac{3}{2}x^{1/2}\)  with \(F(0) = 10\).

jump to solution

Problem 6 - Limits & Continuity (Chapter 2)

Determine the value of \(c\) such that the function \(f(x)\) defined below remains continuous over its domain.  Sketch your resulting function to ensure it is continuous. \[f(x) = \begin{cases}\sqrt{x+1}, \quad &x>-1,\\x^2 + c, & x\leq -1,\end{cases}\]

jump to solution

Problem 7 - The Limit Definition of the Derivative (Chapter 3 - building on Chapter 2)

Use the limit definition of the derivative to find \(f'(x)\) if \(\displaystyle f(x) = \frac{3}{x+1}\). Then find the equation of the tangent line to \(f(x)\) at \(x = 0\).  Graph to check your answer.

jump to solution

Problem 8 - Using Differentiation Rules (Chapter 3)

Using differentiation rules, determine the requested derivative.

a) \(\displaystyle f(x) = \sqrt{x^2 + 2\sin^2(x)}\), find \(f'(x)\)
b) \(\displaystyle x(t) = t\ln(t^2+1)\cos(t)\), find \(\displaystyle \frac{dx}{dt}\)
c) \(\displaystyle x^2 y = y + 2 + xy\sin(x)\), find \(\displaystyle \frac{dy}{dx}\)
d) Lagniappe (bonus): \(\displaystyle x^2 y = y + 2 + xy\sin(x)\), find \(\displaystyle \frac{dx}{dy}\)

jump to solution
